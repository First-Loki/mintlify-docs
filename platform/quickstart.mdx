Lightsail Power is a SaaS platform that brings workspace management, data analytics, and third-party integrations together in one place. This guide walks you through the four essential steps to go from a brand-new account to a running project with teammates on board — so your team can start collaborating and exploring data without delay.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, have the following ready:

- **Admin credentials** for your identity provider (Google Workspace, Okta, Azure AD, etc.) if you plan to enable SSO during setup
- **Email addresses** of the teammates you want to invite in your initial pilot group
- A decision on your **workspace name** — choose something your team will immediately recognize when it appears in notifications and email digests (e.g., `Acme Engineering` rather than `Workspace 1`)
- Your organization's preferred **timezone** and whether new projects should default to **public** (visible to all workspace members) or **private** (invite-only)

<Note>
  If any step in this guide depends on your IT department — such as configuring DNS records, supplying SSO metadata, or opening firewall rules — don't wait. Move ahead with the other steps in parallel and return to the IT-dependent step once your team responds.
</Note>

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Your Organization">
    Sign in to [app.lightsailpower.com](https://app.lightsailpower.com) with your work email. On first login, Lightsail Power detects that no organization exists for your domain and automatically launches the **Organization Setup** prompt.

    1. Click **Create organization** when prompted.
    2. Enter your **organization name** — this is the display name teammates see in notification emails, the browser tab, and shared links. Use a name that is unambiguous at a glance.
    3. Review the **service region** selection. Data residency cannot be changed after creation, so confirm with your compliance team if you have data-sovereignty requirements.
    4. Click **Create** to provision the organization. Lightsail Power generates a unique URL slug (e.g., `acme-engineering.lightsailpower.com`) that anchors all future API calls and workspace URLs.

    Once the spinner resolves, you land on the main dashboard — your organization is live.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure General Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → General** from the left sidebar. These defaults apply automatically to every new member who joins your workspace, so it is worth getting them right before you send any invitations.

    Configure the following fields:

    | Setting | Recommendation |
    |---|---|
    | **Workspace name** | Short, recognizable name for the UI header |
    | **Timezone** | Match your team's primary working timezone |
    | **Date & time format** | Choose ISO 8601 (`YYYY-MM-DD`) for international teams |
    | **Default project visibility** | `Private` is the safer default; open it up per-project as needed |
    | **Display density** | `Comfortable` for most users; `Compact` for data-heavy workflows |

    Click **Save changes** after each section. Changes take effect immediately — no restart or re-login is required.

    <Tip>
      Set the timezone before you invite teammates. Lightsail Power stamps all activity logs and scheduled report deliveries using the workspace timezone, so a mismatch creates confusing timestamps in notifications.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Invite Your Teammates">
    Go to **Settings → Users** and click **Invite members**. Lightsail Power sends each invitee a join link valid for 72 hours.

    For each invite, provide:
    - The teammate's **work email address**
    - Their **workspace role** — choose from the three built-in options:

    | Role | What they can do |
    |---|---|
    | **Admin** | Full access to settings, billing, and all projects |
    | **Member** | Create and manage projects they belong to |
    | **Viewer** | Read-only access to projects they are added to |

    You can invite multiple addresses at once by entering them as a comma-separated list or by uploading a CSV.

    <Warning>
      Start with a **small pilot group** of five to ten people before doing a company-wide rollout. This lets you validate that SSO, notification routing, and project permissions behave as expected before you scale.
    </Warning>

    Once invitees accept and log in, they appear with a green **Active** badge in the Users list. Pending invites show an **Invited** badge and can be resent or revoked at any time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Up Your First Project">
    Return to the **main dashboard** and click **New project** in the top-right corner. Projects are the primary units of work in Lightsail Power — each one has its own members, settings, and data space.

    Fill in the project creation form:

    1. **Name** — use a clear, descriptive name (e.g., `Q3 Sales Analytics` or `Marketing Data Pipeline`). Avoid generic names like `Test` unless this is explicitly a sandbox.
    2. **Description** — one or two sentences explaining the project's purpose. Future teammates will read this when deciding whether to request access.
    3. **Visibility** — set to **Private** for your first project so you can experiment freely.
    4. **Initial members** — add the pilot teammates you invited in Step 3 and assign them project-level roles.

    Click **Create project**. Lightsail Power provisions the project workspace and redirects you to the project dashboard.

    <Info>
      Treat your first project as a **sandbox**. Explore integrations, test data pipelines, and experiment with analytics views here before you standardize naming conventions and data schemas for production projects.
    </Info>
  </Step>
</Steps>
