Lightsail Power Analytics lets you connect data sources, create dashboards, and share insights across your team — all from a single, unified interface. Whether you're pulling from a production database, a data warehouse, or a SaaS tool like Salesforce or Stripe, this guide walks you through every step from your first connection to a live, shareable chart.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have the following ready:

- **Credentials for your data source** — this means a database hostname, username, and password for relational databases, or an API key for SaaS-based sources.
- **Admin or Member role** in your Lightsail Power workspace — Viewer-only accounts cannot create data sources or dashboards.

<Note>
  If your data source sits behind a firewall, add the Lightsail Power outbound IP addresses to your allowlist before running a connection test. Skipping this step will cause the test to time out even when your credentials are correct.
</Note>

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect a data source">
    A data source is the foundation of every dashboard. Start by opening **Analytics** in the left navigation, then go to **Data sources → Add source**.

    1. Select your **source type** from the dropdown — for example, PostgreSQL, Snowflake, or Stripe.
    2. Enter the required **connection details** for your source type (host, port, database name, username, and password, or an API key for SaaS sources).
    3. Click **Test connection**. Lightsail Power validates your credentials and network access. A green checkmark means you're good to go; an error message includes a reason code to help you troubleshoot firewall rules or credential issues.
    4. Once the test passes, click **Save**.

    Your new source now appears in the Data sources list and is ready to query. For a full breakdown of every connection field, supported source types, and sync frequency options, see [Connect a data source](/analytics/connect-data).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a dashboard">
    With a data source saved, you're ready to build your first dashboard. Navigate to **Analytics → Dashboards → New dashboard**.

    Give the dashboard a name that reflects either the data it will display or the team that will use it. Clear, specific names make dashboards easier to discover later — for example:

    - `Weekly revenue`
    - `Support KPIs`
    - `Marketing pipeline – Q3`

    Click **Create** to open the empty dashboard canvas.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a chart">
    Inside your new dashboard, click **Add chart** to open the chart builder.

    1. **Select a data source** — choose the source you connected in Step 1 from the dropdown.
    2. **Choose a chart type** — if you're not sure where to start, bar charts work well for comparisons across categories, line charts suit time-series trends, and number cards highlight a single key metric at a glance.
    3. **Configure your axes** — set the **x-axis** field (typically a date, category, or dimension) and the **y-axis** field (typically a numeric measure or aggregation like `SUM(revenue)` or `COUNT(tickets)`).
    4. Optionally add filters, groupings, or a chart title to give the visualization more context.
    5. Click **Save** to add the chart to your dashboard canvas. Drag and resize it to fit your layout.

    <Tip>
      Start with one or two charts to validate your data is returning what you expect before building out a full dashboard. It's much easier to spot a misconfigured query on a simple chart than inside a complex layout.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share your dashboard">
    Once your dashboard looks the way you want, click **Share** in the dashboard header to open the sharing panel.

    You have two options for sharing:

    - **Copy link** — generates a direct URL you can paste into a Slack message, email, or internal wiki. Use the **Access level** control to decide whether the link is visible to your entire workspace or only to specific members.
    - **Embed code** — generates an `<iframe>` snippet you can drop into an external tool such as Notion, Confluence, or a customer-facing portal. The embedded view respects the same access-level setting.

    <Warning>
      Setting the access level to **Public** makes your dashboard visible to anyone with the link, including people outside your workspace. Only use this setting for data you're comfortable sharing externally.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Next steps

Now that your first dashboard is live, explore what else you can build:

- **Charts & visualizations** — learn every chart type Lightsail Power supports, including scatter plots, funnels, pivot tables, and geographic maps.
- **Scheduled reports** — automatically email a dashboard snapshot to stakeholders on a daily, weekly, or monthly cadence so your team stays informed without logging in.
